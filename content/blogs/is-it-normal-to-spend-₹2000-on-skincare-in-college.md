  You bought a sunscreen because everyone said you need one. Then a face wash.
  Then a moisturizer because the face wash made your skin dry. Somewhere between
  hostel stress, late-night reels, and Nykaa sales, skincare quietly became part
  of your monthly spending.




  And now every time you check your UPI history, there’s another ₹399 you don’t remember spending.




  So we asked students on Boomm:




  **“How much do you actually spend on skincare every month?”**




  The answers felt way more honest than people usually are in real life.




  A lot of people picked somewhere between \*₹1000–₹2500 a month\*. Not because they’re buying luxury skincare. Mostly because small “necessary” purchases keep stacking up.




  A cleanser here. Lip balm there. Sunscreen that finished too fast. One serum you bought after watching a reel at 2am.




  One comment said:


  \> “I thought I barely spent anything till I added everything together 💀”




  Another wrote:


  \> “My parents think all this costs ₹200 total.”




  And honestly, that was *the vibe* across most responses.




  The weird thing about skincare spending in college is everyone acts like they’re low-maintenance while secretly having a full cart saved during every sale.




  Especially between 18 and 21, skincare stops feeling like a luxury and starts feeling like maintenance. Hostel water ruins your skin. College stress shows up on your face. Everyone around you suddenly knows ingredients you’ve never heard before.




  And because most purchases are small, nobody notices how fast the monthly total grows.




  But the poll made one thing clear:


  Most students who think they’re “overspending” on skincare are actually spending around the same amount as everyone else.




  Not cheap enough to feel *casual.*


  Not *expensive* enough to feel rich.


  Just enough to quietly become part of your monthly budget.




  The funny part is nobody really wants skincare advice in these conversations. They just want to know:




  ### “Wait… are other people also spending this much?”




  ### Turns out, yes. A lot of them are.
