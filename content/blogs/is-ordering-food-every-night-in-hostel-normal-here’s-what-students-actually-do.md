  You told yourself today would be different.


  You’d eat mess food. Save money. Be responsible.


  Then 10:47pm happened.


  Someone in the room next to yours ordered rolls. Your friend sent “bro Domino’s?” in the group. Mess dinner was bad. Again.


  Now you’re tracking your order and also wondering:


  ### **“Is ordering food every night in hostel normal… or am I wasting money?”**


  We asked students this exact thing.


  The answers were uncomfortable because almost everyone had the same habit.


  Not every night because they’re rich.




  Every night because sometimes hostel life makes convenience feel cheaper than effort.


  One person said:


  > “I spend more on late-night food than my monthly phone recharge 💀”


  Another wrote:


  > “Started with weekend treats. Became daily survival.”


  And somebody from Delhi NCR:


  > “Swiggy knows my hostel address better than my relatives.”


  Funny thing is, most students underestimate how much they spend on food delivery in college.


  ₹120 momo order.

  ₹180 burger combo.

  ₹250 late-night craving.

  Repeat 15–20 times a month.


  Suddenly **monthly food spending in college** becomes ₹3000–₹7000 without feeling like one big expense.


  That’s where guilt starts.


  Not because of the food.




  Because every order feels small while the total feels embarrassing.


  Especially between 18 and 21, hostel life changes your idea of “normal spending.”


  Your schedule is weird. You skip breakfast. Classes end late. Group ordering becomes social life. Sometimes ordering food isn’t even hunger.


  It’s boredom.


  Stress.


  Assignments.


  Homesickness.


  Or just everyone else ordering.


  And almost nobody talks honestly about that part.


  The weird thing is students rarely want advice like:


  “Cook more.”

  “Manage your budget.”

  “Stop wasting money.”


  Most people just want to know:




  ***“Wait… are other hostel students doing this too?”***


  The answer?


  A lot of them are.


  Maybe not proudly.


  Definitely not publicly.


  But yes.
