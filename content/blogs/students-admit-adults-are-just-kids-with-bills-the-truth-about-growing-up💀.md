  Growing up used to look like achieving complete freedom, staying out late, and
  making your own money. But once you cross into your late teens and early
  twenties, the illusion shatters. You realize that the adults you looked up to
  are largely navigating life through pure guesswork.


  We hosted an "Adulting" themed poll on Boomm asking: "At what age did y’all realise adults are just kids with bills and lore 💀". 


  ### A solid 55% selected "Realised early 🤡", recognizing the truth ahead of time.


  From managing tight monthly pocket money to handling unexpected project deadlines, the learning curve is steep. One local student commented, *"I thought turning 20 meant automatic wisdom. Instead, it just means I have to file my own documents and manage food expenses."*


  The transition can feel isolating, but realizing that everyone else is improvising their adulthood is strangely liberating. You don’t need to have your entire life mapped out by next semester.


  Did you realize this truth early, or are you still processing the shift???
